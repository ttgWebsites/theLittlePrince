Themes

  • Relationships and responsibility
  • The narrator discusses the responsibilities that comes with maintaining successful relationships with other people
  • He remarks that the responsibilities learned through relationships helps strengthens ones idea of responsibility in the world in general
  • The prince's relationship with the rose on his home asteroid is a prime example of this, as the prince is driven to travel to earth to save the rose
  • His deep love is the driving force behind the novel, and through his relationship with the rose he learns a great deal about life
  • The prince learns that what one gives to another is vastly more important than what one receives in return
  • Exploration leads to enlightenment
  • All of the featured characters seem to desire adventure and introspection, or discovering more about one's inner self
  • Through the narrator's experience of meeting the little prince, he discovers much more about himself and the important aspects of life
  • The little prince's travels also tell of self exploration, as he is in constant search of understanding himself spiritually
  • Both the prince and the narrator are constantly exploring, and through that manage to learn more about themselves and their places in the world
  • The downfall of being narrow-minded
  • The Little Prince tells of many different examples of the problems that occur to being narrow-minded
  • A great example is the Turkish man who discovers the prince's asteroid and makes a presentation on it
  • He isn't taken seriously as he is dressed in Turkish clothes, however a few years later he makes the same presentation dressed as a European and receives a very favorable reaction
  • Another example is the flower the prince meets who tells him that earth is very desolate and not many people exist
  • The flower however has lived in the desert so does not know of the populated areas of earth
  • Even the narrator and prince have times of narrow-mindedness
  • The problems with being narrow-minded seem to be the development of prejudices as well stereotypes
  • The reader sees that if one is trapped by narrow-mindedness that, they sacrifice the benefits from constant questioning, and can not lead a truly happy life
